The subtext: Europe wants to co-write the AI playbook alongside allies like India, carving a “third way” between US tech dominance and China’s state-driven model. Direct equity investments and fund-of-funds activity both set records, underscoring the role Bpifrance plays in a cooling macro-funding environment. Net profit dipped from last year but still landed at a solid €501 million. In summary, Bpifrance is a banker, venture capitalist, and industrial strategist all at once. At least until the next presidential election in May 2027. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/finance-marches/banque-assurances/bpifrance-injecte-la-somme-record-de-72-milliards-deuros-dans-leconomie-francaise-2216412?ref=frenchtechjournal.com#utm_medium=Reseaux%20sociaux&utm_campaign=CM_News_Linkedin" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a>, <a href="https://presse.bpifrance.fr/bilan-dactivite-2025-activite-record-72-milliards-deuros-injectes-dans-leconomie-creation-de-valeur-13-milliard-deuros/?lang=fra&ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Bpifrance</strong></a></p><p>🔐🚨 <strong>Major French banking data breach raises red flags.</strong> Hackers accessed the Ficoba national bank account registry via compromised government credentials, exposing data linked to 1.2 million accounts. Since 2019, OSS has launched ~30 B2B industrial software startups (22 still active), now deployed across 3,600 industrial sites. Already known for early bets on Pasqal and Multiverse Computing, Quantonation is broadening its scope to physics-based tech (advanced materials, photonics) as the quantum stack matures. Moral of the story: Hardware is still brutally unforgiving. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/voltaire-angell-la-chute-sans-fin-des-start-up-de-velos-electriques-2216942?ref=frenchtechjournal.com"><strong><u>Les Echos</u></strong></a></p><p>🎤❗France’s media regulator is stepping into podcast territory for the first time, and it’s starting with a bang. ARCOM has opened an investigation into “10 000 pas,” a charting podcast by influencer <strong>Le Raptor</strong>, over episodes containing allegedly homophobic and misogynistic remarks that remain online months after publication. While ARCOM has limited direct authority over podcasts, it’s using the EU’s Digital Services Act as leverage, scrutinizing platforms like Deezer, Spotify, and Apple Music to ensure they provide proper reporting tools and remove illegal content when required. In other words, the spotlight is less on the podcaster and more on the platforms hosting him. If they’re found non-compliant, reminders—and potentially sanctions—could follow. It’s a test case that signals Europe’s content crackdown is expanding beyond social media feeds. | <a href="https://www.lefigaro.fr/secteur/high-tech/l-influenceur-masculiniste-le-raptor-au-coeur-de-la-premiere-instruction-de-l-arcom-ciblant-un-podcast-20260218?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Le Figaro</strong></a></p><p>⚖️📉 <strong>Legaltech chessboard in motion.</strong> French standout Doctrine, fresh from snapping up Spain’s Maite.ai, is reportedly exploring a sale, even though majority owner Summit Partners only came in at €120M in 2023 and was eyeing a 2027 exit. Doctrine’s steady M&amp;A drumbeat (including rival Predictice) has boosted its profile and potentially its price tag. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/french-tech-doctrine-reflechit-a-se-vendre-un-depart-de-station-f-les-confidentiels-de-la-semaine-2216802?ref=frenchtechjournal.com"><strong><u>Les Echos</u></strong></a></p><p>🏥📊 <strong>French healthtech shows quiet resilience.</strong> Despite a tougher funding climate, the sector counts 2,788 companies and saw VC investment rise 15% in 2025, bucking global trends.

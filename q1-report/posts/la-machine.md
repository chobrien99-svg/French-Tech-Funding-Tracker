<h1><center>🟡✨ The Big AI Bonanza</center></h1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p><strong>Howdy, dear readers. </strong></p><p>February in Paris can mean only one thing: artificial intelligence. (What, you were thinking, <em>love? romance? </em>Please. Such analog notions are so 2024.) No, if this month now has a theme, it's AI. And the nation celebrates with AI barometers, ecosystem mappings, closed-door briefings, buzzy conferences at startup campuses, exclusive dinners where the AI hoity-toity can hobnob at the Élysée, and the pending India Impact AI Summit opening in New Delhi on February 16. <a href="https://openthemagazine.com/world/french-president-macron-in-india-next-week-for-ai-summit-talks-with-pm-modi?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>President Macron will be among those traveling to the big AI hoedown next week</strong></a>. </p><p>The conversations this year come with an added splash of existential anxiety as the accelerated pace of AI development made things extra spicy. Is Europe doomed? Is the world doomed? Is this the greatest opportunity of our lifetimes? How do we stop AI? How do we win AI? Is that coffee free? </p><p>Ground zero for the <strong>Great French AI Rodeo </strong>this week was Station F, where France Digitale organized its <a href="https://ai-day.org/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>10th annual AI Day with the PR[AI]RIE AI cluster.</strong></a><strong> </strong>The event took place almost a year to the day after Paris’ big AI summit last year that drew world leaders and corporate big shots to the nation.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/france-digital-day.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1800" height="1206" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/02/france-digital-day.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/02/france-digital-day.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/02/france-digital-day.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/france-digital-day.png 1800w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Photo by David Arous</span></figcaption></figure><p>Chris moderated a panel that included one of the lead organizers of the Paris summit, <strong>Anne Bouverot</strong>, Co-Chair of France’s AI &amp; Digital Council. Joining them was  Business France captain <strong>Pascal Cagni</strong> and <strong>Zebox's Frédéric Guilleux</strong>. They opened by reflecting on progress made since the summit, including €109 billion in public and private commitments to AI spending, the announcement of several high-profile AI startups, a renewed focus on sovereignty, and recognition of France's and Europe's immense talent and potential. </p><p>The big challenges emphasized by both Bouverot and Cagni: big Corporates. While some progress has been made in digital transformation, it remains woefully inadequate. The pair were fairly blunt. AI represents a kind of last chance: If Europe's biggest companies don't seize this opportunity to reinvent themselves, the region risks falling permanently behind. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/Screenshot-2026-02-10-at-22.42.17.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1978" height="1034" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/02/Screenshot-2026-02-10-at-22.42.17.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/02/Screenshot-2026-02-10-at-22.42.17.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/02/Screenshot-2026-02-10-at-22.42.17.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/Screenshot-2026-02-10-at-22.42.17.png 1978w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Panel: How to build AI leaders, not followers in AI</span></figcaption></figure><p>The day also offered a glimpse further down the road, with Yann LeCun outlining a future of AI systems and robots capable of understanding real-world, real-time environments. In conversations on the sidelines, he hinted that this shift from abstract intelligence to situated, embodied AI may move from theory to practice sooner than we think, with more to come in the months ahead.</p><p>On perhaps one of the most operational panels of the day, <strong>Helen</strong> moderated a discussion on <em>moving from individual AI hacks to scalable workflows</em>, with leaders from <strong>Notion, Foundever</strong>,<strong> and Ontomantics</strong>. The takeaway was blunt: AI fails less because of the models and their complexity, and more because organisations don’t redesign workflows, incentives, and accountability early enough. Prompting is easy. Installing scalable AI Operations is hard and unavoidable.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/image-14.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1898" height="956" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/02/image-14.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/02/image-14.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/02/image-14.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/image-14.png 1898w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Panel: From Prompts to scalable works. <p>As part of AI festivities, there have been announcements aplenty. Here now are a few of the tastiest morsels...</p><p>🥩 In a closed-door briefing with the tech press, <strong>Station F</strong> leaders announced the launch of <strong>F/ai</strong>, a new program designed to create the next generation of <strong>global AI champions, </strong>and to do so at a <em>very early stage</em>.</p><p>The program targets <strong>AI-native, early-stage founders only</strong>, selected strictly by recommendation. Out of 70–100 applicants, <strong>just 20 startups have already been chosen, </strong>most less than 12 months old, <strong>80% led by repeat founders</strong>, many with prior exits, PhDs on the team, and <strong>75% already through pre-seed</strong>. Several are either already hitting or aim to hit <strong>€1m in revenue within six months</strong>. Backed by a dense network of <strong>major AI players, including big US tech names, </strong>F/ai offers financing, top-tier mentors, privileged partnerships, and a powerful go-to-market engine. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/visuel-partners-final-1-.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="1125" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/02/visuel-partners-final-1-.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/02/visuel-partners-final-1-.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/02/visuel-partners-final-1-.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w2400/2026/02/visuel-partners-final-1-.png 2400w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>Founders can submit a <em>wish list</em> of corporate intros, with up to five warm connections to accelerate distribution. What stood out this year isn’t just scale, but maturity: GenAI is now mainstream, data quality has become a strategic bottleneck, and agentic, task-specific AI is quietly moving from demos into day-to-day operations. The message echoed across panels and corridors alike: the French AI ecosystem is no longer asking <em>whether</em> AI works but how fast it can scale, industrialize, and secure real access to markets. The barometer’s headline ambition echoed the calls made by Cagni and Bouverot: Getting large European corporates to buy European AI solutions up to just <strong>9% of all purchases within two years. That rather modest goal </strong>landed awkwardly in a room obsessed with strategic autonomy. The message from JFD was clear: AI leadership won’t be regulated into existence. While AI is second nature to the young, only 15% of over-70s use it, turning generative AI into the country’s newest digital divide. | <a href="https://www.20minutes.fr/high-tech/4200704-20260210-adoption-ia-generative-fulgurante-france-selon-credoc?at_medium=RSS+feed&at_campaign=ynews&utm_source=yahoo&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=news_feed&utm_content=read_more_link%22" rel="noreferrer"><strong>20 Minutes</strong></a></p><p>🗞️ <strong>But when it comes to the public sector, GenAI adoption is not so "fulgurant." </strong>France may have written the playbook on “trustworthy AI,” but when it comes to actually using GenAI in public services, it’s mostly stuck in pilot purgatory. The country ranks near the bottom of a new global index among 10 countries, with adoption patchy, tools scarce, and just 27% of public servants saying their organization has invested in AI, according to the report by the Public First for the Center for Data Innovation, sponsored by Google. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/02/index.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="456" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/02/index.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/02/index.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/02/index.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w2400/2026/02/index.png 2400w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>Nearly one in three officials thinks nothing they do could be done by AI, and 45% have never used it at work, which is… not exactly a hotbed of experimentation.

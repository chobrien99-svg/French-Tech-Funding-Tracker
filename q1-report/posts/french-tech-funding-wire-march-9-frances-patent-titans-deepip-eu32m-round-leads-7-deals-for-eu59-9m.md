<H1><center>🧮 Data Point: Patent Titans</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>France’s patent leaderboard is back. <a href="https://www.inpi.fr/ressources/propriete-intellectuelle/palmares-deposants-de-brevet?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>According to the latest ranking</strong></a> from zee <em>Institut National de la Propriété Industrielle (</em>INPI), the patent heavyweights in France remain resolutely industrial. Cars, planes, and the machinery that keeps them moving still rule the innovation game.</p><p>On a macro level, patent activity is rebounding. INPI recorded 16,807 patent applications in 2025, an 8.7% increase year over year, signaling a renewed burst of inventive energy after several years of relative stability. </p><p>At the top of the filing leaderboard sits <strong>Stellantis</strong> with <strong>1,294 patent applications</strong>, followed closely by <strong>Safran (1,266)</strong> and <strong>Renault Group (746)</strong>. In other words, mobility literally drives French innovation, or, at least, by patent filings. The automotive and aerospace giants dominate the list, reflecting the deep R&amp;D pipelines behind electrification, propulsion systems, and next-generation transport technologies.</p><p>After the mobility trio comes an interesting mix of sectors. <strong>L’Oréal (714)</strong> continues to prove that beauty is serious science, while French energy research institute <strong>CEA (568) </strong>anchors the academic side of the innovation ecosystem. Other familiar industrial names fill the top ten: <strong>Valeo, Airbus, Orange, Thales, and STMicroelectronics</strong>.</p><p>Innovation in France is highly concentrated. The top 50 patent filers account for nearly 63% of all filings, while the top 20 alone represent about 53%. In other words, a relatively small club of industrial giants and research institutions carries much of the country’s patent output.</p><p>Despite the AI hype cycle, France’s patent engine still runs on turbines, engines, batteries, and industrial systems.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/03/image.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1090" height="954" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/03/image.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/03/image.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/03/image.png 1090w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><hr><div class="kg-card kg-cta-card kg-cta-bg-blue kg-cta-immersive    kg-cta-centered" data-layout="immersive">

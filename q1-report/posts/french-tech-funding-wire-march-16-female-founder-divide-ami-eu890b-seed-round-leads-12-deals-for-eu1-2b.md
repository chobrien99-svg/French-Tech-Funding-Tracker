<H1><center>🧮 Data Point: Mind The Gap</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>France keeps talking about closing the gender gap in entrepreneurship. And, slowly, <em>very</em> slowly, it’s happening. Women now account for <strong>39% of business creations</strong>, and their startups survive just as well as men’s, with 7 in 10 still alive after five years. This is according to <a href="https://www.entreprises.gouv.fr/files/files/Actualites/2026/barometre-femmes-entrepreneuriat-2026.pdf?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>la 3ème édition du Baromètre sur l’entrepreneuriat des femmes en France</strong></a> published by the Direction générale des Entreprises (DGE) with Bpifrance based on a survey of 5,000 entrepreneurs by Ifop.</p><p>Female micro-entrepreneurs actually outlast their male peers (45% vs. 36%). But the pipeline still leaks: only 23% of women report strong exposure to entrepreneurship, compared with 34% of men, which helps explain why fewer consider launching a company in the first place. Access to money and support also lags, with just 32% of female founders receiving external funding, compared with 40% of men. Some good news: women are inching into tech leadership too, now making up 20% of CEOs or co-founders in the French Tech Next40/120.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/03/women-in-fr-1.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1260" height="1073" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/03/women-in-fr-1.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/03/women-in-fr-1.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/03/women-in-fr-1.png 1260w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><hr><div class="kg-card kg-cta-card kg-cta-bg-blue kg-cta-immersive    kg-cta-centered" data-layout="immersive">

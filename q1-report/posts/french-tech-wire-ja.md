<H1><center>Tech Talk</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>🛒🏷️ The sale of Paris-based AI MarTech startup <a href="https://www.beyable.com/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Beyable</strong></a> to the UK's SaleCycle this month didn't lead to any SCREAMING HEADLINES, in part because the valuation wasn't disclosed, and in part because it doesn't appear that the company ever raised any outside VC. Still, the outcome for Beyable, the artist formerly known as Attuneo, is worth noting for a couple of reasons. First, Beyable was founded in 2014 by Julien Dugaret, Florian Papillon, Saidi Mohamed, and Julien Delhomme. Which, in AI years, makes it approximately 1,500 years old. The company apparently managed to get along just fine for a time, not raising money and just doing its<em> thang</em>. Then, <a href="https://www.pappers.fr/entreprise/attuneo-804353126?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer">according to its filings</a>, it hit some bumps in the road around 2023-2024, where profits fell. The company underwent some kind of restructuring to improve its liquidity and debt situation. From the outside, this may have looked worrisome. However, in reality, the company was in the midst of some kind of generative AI transformation and in October 2023 had been accepted into the <a href="https://www.maddyness.com/2023/12/05/avec-son-generative-ai-startup-program-microsoft-booste-des-startups-qui-veulent-accelerer-lusage-de-lia-generative-au-sein-des-entreprises/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer">first cohort of <strong>Microsoft's Generative AI Startup Studio</strong></a> program in Paris. And now, two years later, it gets snapped up by the Brits. (I wouldn't be surprised if it has raised some kind of Seed round at some point during those 2 years...) By joining forces with SaleCycle, the combined group aims to create a strong European alternative to US-dominated marketing suites, spanning personalization, identity, and full-funnel conversion. In any case, one can choose their own moral of this story. But it was certainly a telling example of a capital-efficient French SaaS success finding scale through smart M&amp;A rather than heavy VC backing. | <a href="https://ww.fashionnetwork.com/news/British-customer-engagement-specialist-salecycle-buys-french-conversion-expert-beyable,1797273.html?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Fashion Network</strong></a>, <a href="https://www.bgf.co.uk/insights/salecycle-acquires-beyable/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>BGF</strong></a>, <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/posts/julien-dugaret-3788961_acquisition-scaleup-ecommerce-activity-7417649571801120768-GGBw?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAAADtQEBK2cPoar2fKhYuhhp-DyTx_pSNBI" rel="noreferrer"><strong>LinkedIn</strong></a></p><p>⚖️👮 Once hailed as a guru of French tech, <strong>The Family</strong> co-founder <strong>Oussama Ammar</strong> is now spending a fair bit of time with prosecutors. France’s financial crimes unit has been investigating him since June 2023 for alleged tax fraud and money laundering, following a complaint from the Ministry of the Economy. That’s on top of a separate judicial probe accusing him of siphoning off millions from The Family, claims pushed by his former partners after his dramatic 2021 exit. Arrested at Nice airport in early 2025, Ammar said he was quickly released, though the legal baggage clearly stayed behind. With courts in the UK and the Cayman Islands already ordering him to pay more than €14 million, the fallen tech star’s empire now looks less like disruption and more like a cautionary tale. | <a href="https://www.maddyness.com/2026/01/14/oussama-ammar-le-co-fondateur-de-the-family-dans-le-viseur-du-parquet-national-financier-pour-fraude-fiscale/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Maddyness</strong></a></p><p>🟢🥬 <strong>Hexa</strong> is officially going green. The startup studio has tapped <strong>Selency founder Maxime Brousse</strong> as Partner to launch <strong><em>Carbon Zero</em></strong>, its first climate-focused cohort under the new Hexa Sprint program, handing out €500k for roughly 10% equity to 12 early-stage startups. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/image-20-1.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1198" height="956" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/image-20-1.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/image-20-1.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/image-20-1.png 1198w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">From left to right, Thibaud Elziere, cofounder @Hexa, and Maxime Brousse, Partner @Hex</span></figcaption></figure><p>After raising €50 million, scaling Selency, and angel-investing in 70+ startups, Brousse is here to get his hands dirty. Carbon Zero will back climate startups that aim to <em>replace</em> legacy systems, not politely optimize them, betting that climate wins only when economics do. | <a href="https://www.hexa.com/cohort/carbon-zero?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Hexa</strong></a></p><p>🤑📲 Speaking of <strong>Hexa</strong>, the artist formerly known as eFounders, one of its firstborn startups, is all grown up. It took <strong>Aircall</strong> a respectable eight years to crawl from zero to $100 million in ARR. Then it apparently found the turbo button. In just three more years, the cloud telephony startup doubled that figure, crossing $200 million in ARR by the end of 2025 and quietly joining the very exclusive club of French Tech “double centaurs.” Now profitable, with 22,000 customers and no urgent need for fresh cash, Aircall is suddenly very zen about its future and much less chatty about that once-hyped Nasdaq IPO. Based now in New York, the company is now piling on AI features, global expansion, and bigger ambitions. Next stop, according to management: $500 million in ARR. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/la-licorne-aircall-franchit-les-200-millions-de-dollars-de-revenus-2209495?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a></p><p><strong>🏦💪 </strong><a href="https://karmen.io/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Karmen</strong></a> bagged a €50m financing line from <strong>Sienna Investment Managers</strong>, proving that in a tightening credit market, institutional money still flows to fintechs that actually lend. This private debt facility is designed to fuel Karmen’s short-term BFR financing for TPEs and SMEs, with larger tickets and broader sector coverage. The deal marks Karmen’s third refinancing in four years and follows its late-2025 takeover of Silvr, underscoring a quiet consolidation underway in alternative SME finance. As banks pull back, fintech lenders with solid underwriting and a bit of institutional patience are picking up the slack. | <a href="https://finyear.com/karmen-leve-50-me-aupres-de-sienna-investment-managers?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Finyear</strong></a></p><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a href="#/portal/signup" class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent">Become a Paid Subscriber</a></div><hr>

<H1><center>French VC Outlook 2026: More concentration, Fewer Bets, Bigger Stakes</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><a href="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/french-vc-outlook-2026-more-concentration-fewer-bets-bigger-stakes/"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/image-22.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1164" height="780" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/image-22.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/image-22.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/image-22.png 1164w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></a></figure><p>French VC is entering 2026 with fewer deals, bigger checks, and far less room for error. </p><p>After a bruising 2025 marked by political chaos and thinning early-stage funding, capital is concentrating into fewer funds, fewer startups, and much higher-stakes bets. AI still dominates, but the mood has shifted from flashy apps to hard infrastructure, defence, sovereignty, and energy resilience. Beneath the headline fundraising numbers, several top investors warn the system is more fragile than it looks. </p><p>Buckle up: this is the year where conviction replaces optimism, and mistakes get expensive.

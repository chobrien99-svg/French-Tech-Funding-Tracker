<H1><center>Tech Talk</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>💸 📉 <strong>French Tech M&amp;A Hit a Wall in 2025.</strong> Deal volume rose 15% last year, but deal value plummeted 73% to €4.1B, back to 2020 levels. Only six mega-deals topped €100M, including Gojob and Younited. IPOs were practically extinct (just one: Younited via SPAC). Entrepreneurs are cashing out, happy with €20 - €30M exits, but the ecosystem misses the mega-moves that recycle capital. Growth equity funds are stepping in to consolidate promising startups, but don’t expect fireworks in 2026: political uncertainty and election season loom. French M&amp;A might be busy…just don’t call it booming. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/french-tech-le-grand-effondrement-des-fusions-acquisitions-2207807?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a></p><p>👋 💼 <strong>Fanny Moizant Exits Vestiaire Collective After 16 Years. </strong>The cofounder of the French luxury resale platform leaves following recent organizational changes, a move she describes as neither expected nor chosen. "I am leaving the company as part of the recent organizational changes. This was not a decision I initiated, nor one I expected, but I accept that it marks the end of an extraordinary chapter," <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/posts/fanny-moizant-40665322_after-16-years-its-time-for-me-to-say-activity-7413987665278156800-E-0M/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>she wrote on LinkedIn</strong></a>. Moizant’s exit comes as new <strong>CEO Bernard Osta</strong> pushes international expansion (hello, US &amp; Asia!) and ramps up AI for buyers and sellers. Vestiaire, valued at €1.1B in 2023 and still unprofitable, had shelved IPO plans in a tough market. <strong>Moizant</strong> added, "As for me, I’m OK. Through this difficult chapter, I received an unexpected gift: it helped me truly understand that 'In the depth of winter, I finally learned that within me there lay an invincible summer.' (Albert Camus) I don’t know exactly what comes next yet, but I move forward with energy, resilience, and fire in my belly." | <a href="https://www.maddyness.com/2026/01/08/fanny-moizant-quitte-vestiaire-collective-apres-seize-ans/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Maddyness</strong></a><strong>, </strong><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/posts/fanny-moizant-40665322_after-16-years-its-time-for-me-to-say-activity-7413987665278156800-E-0M/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>LinkedIn</strong></a></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/Screenshot-2026-01-08-at-18.43.36.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1540" height="924" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/Screenshot-2026-01-08-at-18.43.36.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/Screenshot-2026-01-08-at-18.43.36.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/Screenshot-2026-01-08-at-18.43.36.png 1540w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>👨‍⚖️⚖️ <strong>A Paris court</strong> on Monday delivered a long-overdue reality check to the online rumor mill, convicting 10 people for cyberbullying France’s first lady, <strong>Brigitte Macron</strong>, over grotesque false claims about her gender and sexuality. The defendants—aged 41 to 65—earned themselves prison sentences (some suspended), mandatory cyberbullying training, temporary social media bans, and a collective bill of nearly $12,000 in damages. French construction and infrastructure startups are swapping flashy apps for boots-on-the-ground innovation.</strong> Leonard, VINCI’s innovation platform, says the new wave focuses on decarbonization, safety, and productivity, with AI embedded directly into equipment, machines, and materials, not just dashboards. Startups like HyLight (drones instead of helicopters), Strong By Form (low-carbon composites), and Ostrea (recycled shells) are turning regulations and climate challenges into business opportunities. <strong>Launch Day, January 14, will showcase 50 of these field-ready solutions. Once constrained by Sony and slowed by China exposure, Prophesee has refocused on defense and industrial markets, where its event-based “metavision” sensors—modeled on the human retina—are now detecting drones and inspecting factories instead of chasing smartphones. With roughly 70% of revenue coming from industrial clients and defense traction accelerating, the company is trimming risk, dialing back consumer ambitions, and aiming for profitability next year.<strong> | </strong><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/posts/luca-verre-71b6a75_leadership-entrepreneurship-deeptech-activity-7408730799233011712-6da8/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAAAoWBUByiC6UTn6dT35SuzmACQGihSrY3g" rel="noreferrer"><strong>LinkedIn</strong></a></p><p>🛠️ 💼 <strong>Sociologist Marion Flécher argues that the startup world doesn’t overthrow capitalism...it dresses it in hoodies</strong>. Her research shows that while startups flatten hierarchies and preach autonomy, they introduce new forms of control: employees are nudged to overcommit, self-discipline, and even overperform. The result is loyal, engaged teams… but also porous work-life boundaries, burnout risks, and silent suffering. Startups may look “cool,” but behind the ping-pong tables, the rules of capitalism still apply, just with a friendlier face. | <a href="https://www.lemonde.fr/emploi/article/2026/01/08/marion-flecher-sociologue-loin-de-remettre-en-cause-le-capitalisme-les-start-up-en-proposent-un-nouveau-visage_6660953_1698637.html?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Le Monde</strong></a></p><p>💸🚗 <strong>Blablacar SPV at 90% Discount. </strong>According to our friends at Les Echos, a French Tech entrepreneur offered their <strong>Blablacar SPV shares at a 90% discount</strong> in a December email to a handful of investors — only if the deal closed before December 29. The motive was likely tax-driven, in their opinion. Such unicorn discounts are increasingly common for 2021–22-era tech darlings; raise new funds today, and most would probably miss their “unicorn moment.” | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/tech-medias/hightech/soitec-nomme-un-nouveau-patron-pour-redresser-la-barre-2208378?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a> </p><p>💹 🌍 <strong>Akeneo Posts Strong Growth, Still in the Red.</strong> The Nantes-based product info specialist grew revenue <strong>23% to €63.4M in 2024</strong> (from €51.4M in 2023) while narrowing losses to €13M (versus €29.5M). With <strong>400 employees</strong> (60% in France) and subsidiaries from the US to Germany, the company completed its 2023 acquisition of <strong>Unifai</strong> to strengthen data reliability. Despite growth, profitability remains elusive, but international expansion and solid funding (€135M in 2022) signal ambition. | <a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/french-tech-les-revenus-dakeneo-un-family-office-parti-au-luxembourg-les-confidentiels-de-la-semaine-2208389?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a></p><p>⚖️ 🚀 <strong>European Rules will Hit French Startups in 2026. </strong>Brace yourself: the <strong>AI Act</strong> kicks in August (high-risk AI systems, transparency, and innovation sandboxes), the <strong>Data Act</strong> makes real-time access to connected-device data mandatory by September, and the <strong>Cyber Resilience Act</strong> adds new security reporting obligations starting this year. Add the proposed <strong>28th regime</strong> for startups (think “European Delaware”), <strong>NIS2</strong> for cybersecurity, <strong>electronic invoicing</strong>, and <strong>pay transparency</strong> rules, and you’ve got enough regulation to make any founder double-check their compliance checklist. The advice is to stay informed, start early, and maybe invest in a good lawyer. |<strong> </strong><a href="https://www.lesechos.fr/start-up/ecosysteme/28e-regime-ai-act-data-act-ces-nouvelles-reglementations-qui-vont-toucher-les-start-up-2207730?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Les Echos</strong></a></p><hr>

<H1><center>🧮 Data Point: CES 2026</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>France once again sent a major startup delegation to CES 2026 in Las Vegas, with at least 114 French tech companies exhibiting across 16 sectors, from AI and robotics to health and green tech. Despite the high costs, French officials and founders argue that CES remains unmatched for rapid global exposure and deal-making. The delegation is smaller than in pre-pandemic years, down from more than 300 startups in 2018–2019, reflecting tighter funding and a tougher U.S. market. Notably, 80% of the French startups this year were CES veterans, signaling a strategic shift toward experienced companies with market-ready products. <H1><center> 💸 French Tech Funding 2025</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><a href="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/french-tech-funding-2025-ai-dominance-82b-raised/"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-2.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1870" height="1324" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-2.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-2.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-2.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-2.png 1870w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></a></figure><p>France's startup ecosystem recorded 686 funding rounds throughout 2025, collectively raising €8.2 billion, according to data tracked by The French Tech Journal.</p><p>This comprehensive dataset, covering deals from January through December, is available via our new database, which allows you to sort the data by company, geography, and sector.</p><p><strong><em>This first version of the database only allowed each company to be tagged with a single sector. So to get a fuller picture of a sector like "cybersecurity" or "foodtech," it's better to search under related terms such as "food." Version 2 will place companies in multiple categories for easier sorting.</em></strong></p><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a href="https://funding2025.frenchtechjournal.com/?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent">Test drive the French Tech Funding 2025 Database</a></div><p>Some key highlights from the data:</p><ul><li><strong>AI and Machine Learning represented 62.5% of deal volume</strong></li><li><strong>Clean Tech and Energy were second at 14.5%</strong></li><li><strong>Average deal size was €12M.</strong></li><li><strong>Outside of Paris, Lyon had the most startups raise (34), but Toulouse had the highest deal value (€322M)</strong>, which allows you to sort the data by company, geography, and sector</li></ul><h2 id="ai-takes-center-stage">AI Takes Center Stage</h2><p>Coming back to France, AI is also the engine driving the VC funding train. It accounted for €5.18B or 62.5% of all funding in 2025.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-1-1.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="850" height="767" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-1-1.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/french-tech-funding-2025-1-1.png 850w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>Out of that, Mistral AI represented the lion's share. Take away Mistral, and the year in French tech funding looks very different, which is again why I don't think it's worth fussing too much with the "up or down" issue. If you are not a giant LLM, life looks very different at the other end of the funding telescope.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/Largest-Deals-2025.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="548" height="808"></figure><p>Still, the good news is that French AI companies are attracting substantial capital, not just early-stage experimentation. The data indicates that France has moved beyond simply participating in the AI revolution to becoming a meaningful player in the space.</p><p>This likely won't stop the ongoing debate among founders and VCs about whether they need to relocate to San Francisco, but with some of the recent high-profile AI startup launches in Paris, France is certainly demonstrating some resilience.</p><h2 id="cleantech-and-health-strong-secondary-sectors">CleanTech and Health: Strong Secondary Sectors</h2><p>Beyond AI, two sectors showed substantial activity:</p><ul><li>CleanTech and Energy companies raised €979.4 million (11.9%) across 109 deals, making it the second-largest sector by both deal count and total funding. With an average round size of €8.98 million, these companies are attracting meaningful capital as France positions itself as a leader in the energy transition.</li><li>HealthTech and BioTech companies completed 56 funding rounds totaling €496.9 million (6.1%). The sector's average round size of €8.9 million suggests that investors see significant potential in French health innovation. This is particularly relevant given Europe's strong position in medical research and the ongoing digitization of healthcare.</li></ul><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a href="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/french-tech-funding-2025-ai-dominance-82b-raised/" class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent">Read the 2025 French tech funding analysis</a></div><hr>

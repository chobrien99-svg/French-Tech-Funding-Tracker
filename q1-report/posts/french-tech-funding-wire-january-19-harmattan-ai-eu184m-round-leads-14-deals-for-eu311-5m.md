<H1><center>🧮 Data Point: 2025 Exit Blues</center></H1>
<!--kg-card-end: html-->
<p>Avolta's <a href="https://mailchi.mp/avolta.io/french-tech-trends-2025?ref=frenchtechjournal.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>FrenchTech Trends &amp; Multiples&nbsp;2025</strong></a> tracked with most year-end wrap-ups in terms of VC investment numbers for Startup Nation. But the firm also has some Exit Data that reinforces that France's struggles in this domain, alas, remain all too real. </p><p>The advisory firm reported <strong>408 exit transactions with disclosed exit value declined to €5.3bn.</strong> The number of deals was up, but the value was down, to the "absence of mega-deals this year," according to Avolta.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/Avolta.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="1123" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/Avolta.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/Avolta.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/01/Avolta.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/Avolta.png 2226w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>When it comes to exit, France is still playing small ball. Most exits are valued below €50m. International acquirers are capturing 80% of the exit value and 40% of exit volume, according to Avolta. Strategic acquisitions are the most common exits. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/avolta-1.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="1123" srcset="https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w600/2026/01/avolta-1.png 600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1000/2026/01/avolta-1.png 1000w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/size/w1600/2026/01/avolta-1.png 1600w, https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/content/images/2026/01/avolta-1.png 2226w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>Of course, this is hardly a shocker. </p><p>Late last year, La French Tech Mission launched a national consultation on exits, acknowledging that while the French startup ecosystem has matured, it still struggles with the lack of clear and frequent exit opportunities. French Tech Director Julie Huguet argued that without more visible exits, venture capital recycling slows down and France risks losing key technologies and jobs to non-European buyers.

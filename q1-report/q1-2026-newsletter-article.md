# The Great Concentration: French Tech Raised €2.73B in Q1 — Fewer Bets, Bigger Stakes

Here's a number that should make you sit up: **€2.73 billion**. That's how much French tech startups raised in Q1 2026 — January through March — according to data from our [French Tech Funding Tracker](https://funding.frenchtechjournal.com). It's a 79% jump from the €1.53 billion raised in Q1 2025. By any measure, that's a blockbuster opening quarter.

But before you pop the champagne, consider this one: **139**. That's the total number of deals. Down from 189 in Q1 2025. A 27% decline.

So what's going on? More money, fewer deals. The average round more than doubled — from €8.3M to €20.4M. The median nearly doubled too, from €2.5M to €4.7M. In other words: investors are concentrating their capital into fewer, higher-conviction bets. Welcome to what we're calling **The Great Concentration**.

## The AI Elephant in the Room

Let's get the obvious out of the way. AI & Machine Learning captured an astonishing **€1.83 billion** — fully 67% of all Q1 funding. That's up from €314M in Q1 2025. A 483% increase. Nearly every major deal this quarter had AI somewhere in its DNA.

And yes, one deal looms very, very large. In March, Yann LeCun's **Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI)** launched with an **€890M seed round** — Europe's largest ever — at a €3.5B pre-money valuation, with 67+ investors piling in over just 2.5 weeks. That single deal accounts for a third of the entire quarter's total.

So is this just the AMI show? We ran the numbers without it. Strip out that €890M and Q1 2026 still comes in at **€1.84 billion** — a healthy **+20.5% over Q1 2025**. The average round is still up 68%. The underlying market is genuinely growing, not just inflated by one mega-round. This isn't the dip many feared after 2025's slowdown. It's a different landscape, but not a distressed one.

## March Rewrote the Quarter

January and February were remarkably flat against 2025: roughly €777M and €368M respectively, tracking almost identically to the prior year on funding, though with fewer deals.

Then March happened. **€1.59 billion in a single month** — more than the *entire* Q1 2025. AMI's €890M led the charge, but Hynaero (€117M for amphibious firefighting aircraft — yes, really), Alan (€100M at a €5B valuation), and a dozen others piled on.

## The Sectors That Matter

Beyond AI's gravitational pull, the sector story is fascinating.

**Energy** went from €8M in Q1 2025 to **€202M** — a 23x increase. newcleo is building next-gen nuclear reactors (€75M), Calogena is doing nuclear-powered district heating (€52M), and Jimmy Energy raised €40M for industrial nuclear micro-reactors. France's energy sovereignty ambitions are no longer just policy papers — they're venture-scale investments.

**DefenseTech and ClimateTech** didn't exist as categories in our Q1 2025 data. In Q1 2026 they accounted for €145M and €164M respectively. Hynaero bridges both: its Fregate-F100 water bombers address wildfire suppression (climate) but the aerospace engineering is inherently dual-use (defense). EGIDE raised €8M for anti-drone systems. Aerix Systems pulled in €5M for omnidirectional defense robots. Geopolitics is generating dealflow.

**Cybersecurity** surged from €11M to €133M (+1,124%). GitGuardian's €42M growth round, Qevlar AI's €26M for autonomous security investigation, and Stoïk's €20M cyber-insurance play are riding a wave of post-geopolitical tension security spending and EU regulatory pressure.

## Growth Stage: The Quiet Revolution

Here's a number that deserves more attention: **€640M in growth-stage deals**, up from €143M in Q1 2025. That's a 349% increase across 19 rounds. Pennylane (€175M), Alan (€100M), DentalMonitoring (€84M), GitGuardian (€42M) — these are French companies raising serious late-stage capital without decamping to Sand Hill Road.

This matters structurally. For years, the knock on European tech was that companies had to go to the U.S. for growth capital. Q1 2026 suggests that equation is shifting. Whether it sticks is a Q2 question, but the early signal is encouraging.

## The Pipeline Paradox

Not everything is rosy. **Series A deals nearly halved** — from 41 to 22 — even as funding dropped 17%. Pre-seed is healthy (14 deals, up from 11), meaning new companies are forming. But the middle of the funnel is thinning. Bpifrance flagged this in February, warning that despite record headline numbers, the structural pipeline has gaps.

This is the tension at the heart of Q1: record capital deployed alongside a contracting deal pipeline. More money, fewer bets. The companies that do raise are raising more than ever. The ones that don't... well, we'll see how that plays out.

## Paris and Everyone Else

Paris commanded **77% of funding** but only **50% of deals** — meaning the average Paris round (€30M) was more than 3x the average outside Paris (€9.2M). But 44 cities hosted funded startups. Bordeaux punched above its weight with €156M (Hynaero + FineHeart). Lyon contributed €64M across 7 deals. Aix-en-Provence delivered €94M from its energy cluster.

The long tail of French innovation is alive. It just doesn't have Paris-sized cheques.

## What We're Watching

AMI's execution. The Series A squeeze. Whether growth-stage companies can hit revenue milestones that justify their valuations. The continued emergence of DefenseTech and sovereign tech. And, always, the European scale-or-sell dilemma — brought into sharp relief this quarter by Gleamer's €230M sale to U.S. giant RadNet and Pasqal's $2B SPAC leap to Nasdaq.

Q1 2026 wasn't a recovery story. It was a restructuring story. The money is here. The question is whether the ecosystem can build the pipeline to absorb it.

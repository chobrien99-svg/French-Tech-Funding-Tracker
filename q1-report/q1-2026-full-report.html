Quarterly Intelligence Briefing · January–March 2026

The Great
Concentration

French tech startups raised €2.73 billion in Q1 2026—a 79% surge from the same period in 2025. But beneath that headline: deal count fell 27%. Investors are making fewer, bigger, more concentrated bets. AI captured two-thirds of all capital. Three mega-deals accounted for nearly half the quarter.

New sectors materialized—DefenseTech, ClimateTech—while others faded. The growth stage boomed. The early-stage pipeline thinned. This is not a recovery story. It is a restructuring of the French tech landscape.

The AMI Moment

Yann LeCun’s Advanced Machine Intelligence launched in March with €890M in seed funding—Europe’s largest-ever seed round—at a €3.5B pre-money valuation with 67+ investors. AMI aims to build “world models” that understand physical reality, moving beyond large language models. The round closed in just 2.5 weeks, signaling extraordinary investor conviction in France’s AI ambitions.

AI: Signal vs. Noise

AI captured €1.83B of Q1’s €2.73B total. But even without AMI’s €890M, AI funding was €943M—still triple Q1 2025’s €314M. AI isn’t just one mega-deal; it is the gravitational center of the entire ecosystem. Nearly every top-10 company has AI in its value proposition.

The Growth-Stage Story

Growth rounds surged from €143M to €640M—up 349%. Nineteen companies raised growth-stage capital, compared to just 11 in Q1 2025. Companies like Pennylane (€175M), Alan (€100M), and DentalMonitoring (€84M) are scaling at home rather than seeking U.S. rounds. This is a meaningful structural shift: French startups can now raise late-stage capital without leaving Europe.

The Pipeline Paradox

Pre-seed is healthy (14 deals, up from 11). But Series A shrank from 41 deals to 22—a 46% drop. This “missing middle” is the gap between early formation and later-stage scale. Bpifrance flagged this risk in February, warning that despite record headline numbers, the structural pipeline is thinning. Watch this space in Q2.

Not Catastrophic—A Different Landscape

Even without AMI, Q1 2026 funding is up 20% year-over-year. The average round is still 68% larger. Growth-stage deals surged regardless of AMI. The story isn’t a market in distress—it’s a market restructuring. Fewer companies are raising, but those that do are raising significantly more. The growth-stage figures are particularly encouraging: 19 deals totaling €640M suggests French companies are finding scale capital at home, contradicting the alarm Bpifrance sounded in February about structural gaps.

The concern remains at Series A, where deal count nearly halved. But the overall trajectory—more capital deployed, bigger rounds, healthy growth stage—points to a maturing market, not a contracting one.

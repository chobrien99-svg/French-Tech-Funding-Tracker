# French Tech Funding: Q1 2026 Analysis
### Data-driven briefing for the Q1 2026 Report | April 2026

> **Note on data coverage:** Data entry for 2026 began on January 16, so some early-January deals may be underrepresented in the Q1 2026 totals compared to Q1 2025.

---

## Executive Summary

French tech startups raised **€2.73 billion** across **139 deals** in Q1 2026 (January–March), a **78.6% increase in total funding** compared to the same period in 2025 (€1.53B, 189 deals) — despite a **26.5% decline in deal count**. The average round size more than doubled, jumping from €8.3M to **€20.4M (+147%)**. The median round also nearly doubled from €2.5M to **€4.7M**.

The headline: fewer but much larger deals, led by a massive AI wave and the emergence of defense and climate tech as meaningful categories.

### Key Sector Trends

1. **AI dominance is unprecedented.** AI & Machine Learning attracted €1.83B in Q1 2026 — 67% of all Q1 funding — up from €314M (21%) in Q1 2025. Nearly every top deal has an AI component. The Advanced Machine Intelligence €890M seed round alone exceeds all of Q1 2025's AI funding.

2. **Energy is the breakout sector.** From just €8M in Q1 2025 to €202M in Q1 2026, driven by newcleo (€75M nuclear), Calogena (€52M), and Jimmy Energy (€40M). France's energy transition ambitions are attracting serious capital.

3. **ClimateTech and DefenseTech emerge as new categories.** Neither registered deals in Q1 2025; in Q1 2026 they account for €164M and €145M respectively. Hynaero (€117M) is tagged both SpaceTech and DefenseTech, signaling dual-use aerospace investment.

4. **Cybersecurity is surging.** €11M → €133M (+1,124%), led by GitGuardian (€42M) and Qevlar AI (€26M). Post-geopolitical tensions and EU regulatory pressure are driving security spending.

5. **E-commerce & Retail is fading.** Down 67% from €95M to €31M, and deal count halved (22 → 11). Investors are rotating away from consumer commerce toward deep-tech and enterprise.

6. **Mobility collapsed.** From €37M across 8 deals down to just €1M and 1 deal — a stark reversal.

| **Series B** | €469M | €525M | +12% | 16 | 13 |
| **Series A** | €426M | €353M | -17% | 41 | 22 |
| **Series C** | €173M | €55M | -68% | 2 | 2 |
| **Pre-Seed** | €7M | €27M | +274% | 11 | 14 |

### Key Stage Trends

1. **Seed rounds are the story.** Seed funding exploded from €239M to €1.12B — but this is heavily skewed by the Advanced Machine Intelligence €890M "seed" round. Without it, seed would be €233M, roughly flat. However, median seed deal sizes are still up.

2. **Growth-stage is booming.** From €143M (11 deals) to €640M (19 deals). Companies like Pennylane (€175M), Alan (€100M), DentalMonitoring (€84M) are raising very large late-stage rounds. This suggests more companies are scaling domestically rather than seeking US rounds.

3. **Series A is contracting.** Down 17% in volume and nearly halved in deal count (41 → 22). This could indicate a tougher Series A market, or simply that fewer companies are at that stage in the pipeline.

4. **Pre-Seed is healthy.** Deal count actually grew (11 → 14), and total volume nearly quadrupled. Early-stage formation remains active.

## Key Narratives for the Report

### 1. "The AI Quarter"
AI touched almost every major deal. Even companies not primarily categorized as AI (Pennylane, DentalMonitoring, Qevlar) are AI-enhanced. The €890M Advanced Machine Intelligence seed round is the single largest seed in French tech history and signals France's ambition to compete at the frontier of AI development.

### 2. "Fewer Deals, Bigger Bets"
Investors are concentrating capital into higher-conviction positions. Deal count dropped 27% but total funding rose 79%. The average round more than doubled. This mirrors a global trend of capital consolidation, but France's increase is particularly dramatic.

### 3. "New Sectors Emerge"
DefenseTech (€145M from zero) and ClimateTech (€164M from zero) didn't register in Q1 2025 data at all. France's strategic priorities — defense sovereignty, climate transition — are now generating venture-scale investment.

### 4. "Energy Renaissance"
The 23x growth in energy deals reflects France's nuclear and clean energy ambitions. newcleo (nuclear, €75M), Calogena (geothermal, €52M), and Jimmy Energy (€40M) represent diverse bets across the energy stack.

### 5. "Paris Gravity, Regional Vitality"
While Paris dominates by total euros, the 44 cities with funded startups show ecosystem breadth. Bordeaux (Hynaero, FineHeart), Lyon (Verley), and Aix-en-Provence (Calogena, SunLib) are emerging as secondary hubs for deep-tech and energy.

### 6. "Consumer Out, Enterprise In"
E-commerce/retail (-67%) and mobility (-96%) collapsed. The market has rotated decisively toward enterprise software, security, health, and deep-tech — sectors with clearer paths to durable revenue.

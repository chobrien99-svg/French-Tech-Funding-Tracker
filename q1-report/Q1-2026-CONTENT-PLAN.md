## Recommended Theme for the Introduction

### "The Great Concentration"

Q1 2026 tells one story two ways. The money surged — €2.73 billion, up 79% from Q1 2025 — but the deal count dropped 27%. Investors are making fewer, bigger, more concentrated bets. AI captured 67% of all funding. Three mega-deals (AMI, Hynaero, Pennylane) accounted for nearly half the quarter's total. New sectors like DefenseTech and ClimateTech materialized from nothing. Old ones like e-commerce and mobility nearly vanished.

This is not a recovery story. It's a restructuring story. French tech is concentrating — around AI, around Paris, around fewer companies that are raising more money than ever. The question the report should pose: **Is this concentration a sign of strength, or a warning?**

Your own coverage this quarter reflects this tension beautifully. You wrote about the AMI €890M seed round, the biggest in European history. But you also wrote about NAAREA burning through €90M and collapsing. About Bpifrance sounding the alarm even as records break. About deeptech seed funding stalling. About the "Scale or Sell" dilemma that Gleamer faced. About the capital gap that still haunts Europe's growth stage.

The intro should weave together the triumph (€2.73B, AI explosion, Yann LeCun's AMI moment) with the tension (fewer bets, a thinning pipeline, the sovereignty question).

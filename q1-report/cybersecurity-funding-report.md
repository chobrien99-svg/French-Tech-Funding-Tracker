# French Cybersecurity Funding: From Niche to National Priority
### A sector-by-sector analysis of cybersecurity deals in 2025 and Q1 2026

---

## Executive Summary

French cybersecurity startups raised **€133.3M across 8 deals in Q1 2026** — a **12x increase** over Q1 2025's €10.9M from 6 deals. For the full year 2025, the sector logged **€115.6M across 15 deals**. In other words, Q1 2026 alone has already exceeded the entire previous year.

This isn't a blip. It reflects a structural shift: cybersecurity has moved from a secondary category in French tech funding to a frontline investment thesis, driven by escalating geopolitical tensions, the EU AI Act's compliance mandates, and the explosive growth of AI-powered attack surfaces.

Crucially, nearly every funded company in this space is either already operating in the United States or has explicitly earmarked its new capital for U.S. expansion.

## Q1 2026: The Deals

### 1. GitGuardian — €42.1M (Growth, February, Paris)
**Secrets detection and non-human identity security**

GitGuardian specializes in detecting exposed secrets (API keys, tokens, credentials) and protecting non-human identities (NHIs) across code repositories and CI/CD pipelines. The company's growth round positions it as a key player in securing the proliferation of AI agents — every agent needs credentials, and every credential is an attack surface.

**U.S. presence:** GitGuardian has had a significant U.S. customer base and presence from early on, with the developer security market heavily concentrated in North America.

### 2. Qevlar AI — €26.2M (Series B, March, Issy-les-Moulineaux)
**Autonomous AI SOC platform**

Qevlar builds AI agents that automate security operations center (SOC) investigations — reducing investigation time to roughly 3 minutes per alert, a claimed 10x improvement over traditional workflows. The company uses a hybrid AI approach (graph AI, Bayesian modeling, classical ML) rather than pure LLMs, prioritizing consistency over raw generative capability.

Customers include Mercedes-Benz, Sodexo, MediaMarkt, Atos, and Orange Cyberdefense. Total funding now stands at $44M after this round and a $14M raise in April 2025.

**U.S. expansion:** Qevlar has existing U.S. customers and is actively building out its American presence. Co-founder Ahmed Achchak noted that successful cybersecurity companies have historically come from Israel or the U.S., and positioning Qevlar in that market is a core strategic priority. As he told us: "We want people who are ready to prove the stats wrong."

*We profiled Qevlar in depth: ["Putting Out the Fire and Finding What Started It"](https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/putting-out-the-fire-and-finding-what-started-it-qevlar-ais-bet-on-autonomous-security-intelligence/)*

### 3. Stoïk — €20.0M (Series C, January, Paris)
**Integrated cyber insurance, prevention, and incident response**

Stoïk combines cyber insurance with proactive security monitoring and 24/7 incident response, targeting SMEs and mid-sized businesses. The company has over 10,000 insured companies, nearly €50M in gross written premiums in 2025, and operations in six European countries. The round was Stoïk's fifth fundraise in five years, with backing from Impala, Opera Tech Ventures, Alven, and Andreessen Horowitz.

**U.S. perspective:** CEO Jules Veyrat told us the company's immediate focus is pan-European ("The UK was already extremely crowded with US players. Our outlook has always been European.") but does not rule out U.S. expansion in the 3-5 year horizon. Stoïk's ambition: to become "the CISO of Europe."

*We profiled Stoïk in depth: ["Becoming Europe's CISO: Stoïk's €20M Series C plan"](https://www.frenchtechjournal.com/stoik-ciso-20m-euros/)*

### 4. Escape — €15.4M (Series A, March, Paris)
**AI-native offensive security engineering**

Escape automates vulnerability discovery, testing, and remediation using AI agents that simulate attacker behavior in live production environments. The company serves 2,000+ security teams globally and conducts over 300,000 automated security assessments monthly. Backed by Balderton Capital, Y Combinator, IRIS, and Uncorrelated Ventures.

**U.S. expansion:** As a Y Combinator alumnus with global enterprise clients, Escape has deep U.S. ties. The funding will support international growth, with North America a primary target market for offensive security tooling.

### 5. Meelo — €14.0M (Series B, February)
**Digital onboarding security and fraud prevention**

Meelo provides SaaS-based fraud prevention and risk intelligence for digital onboarding — preventing identity fraud, document forgery, and financial crime at the point of customer acquisition.

### 6. Symbiotic Security — €10.0M (Seed, January, New York/Paris)
**AI-generated secure code**

Symbiotic Security is building the first AI code generation agent designed to produce secure code by default. Its product, Symbiotic Code, embeds security directly into the code generation process — a category-defining bet on securing the output of AI coding assistants. The company is dual-headquartered in New York and Paris.

**U.S. presence:** Already headquartered in New York. The company was founded with a transatlantic model from day one, targeting the U.S. enterprise developer market.

### 7. Lupin & Holmes — €5.4M (Pre-Seed, March, Paris)
**Real-world attack path mapping**

Lupin & Holmes develops Depi, a platform that maps real-world attack paths across software systems by analyzing how multiple vulnerabilities can be combined — shifting security from static detection to dynamic exploitation intelligence.

### 8. Smartphone iD — €0.2M (Seed, January, Paris)
**AI-powered identity verification**

French startup reinventing identity photo and remote identity verification using proprietary AI biometric technology.

## Key Trends

### 1. AI is both the threat and the solution
Every major Q1 2026 cyber deal involves AI on the product side — GitGuardian securing AI agents' credentials, Qevlar using hybrid AI for SOC automation, Escape using AI agents for offensive security, Symbiotic embedding security into AI code generation. The sector has moved from "cybersecurity + AI" to "cybersecurity *because of* AI." As AI agents proliferate across enterprise workflows, the attack surface is expanding exponentially.

### 2. The U.S. market is the primary growth vector
Of the 8 Q1 2026 deals:
- **Symbiotic Security** is headquartered in New York
- **Qevlar AI** has existing U.S. customers and is building out its American presence
- **GitGuardian** has deep U.S. developer community penetration
- **Escape** (YC alumnus) targets North America as a primary market
- **MokN** (2025) raised specifically for U.S. deployment

The pattern is clear: French cybersecurity startups build product in Paris but sell in America. The U.S. remains the world's largest cybersecurity market, and French companies are increasingly viable competitors there — not just as European alternatives, but as best-in-class products.

### 3. From seed to scale: the sector is maturing
In 2025, 11 of 15 deals were seed rounds. In Q1 2026, the mix is dramatically different: 1 Growth round, 2 Series B, 1 Series C, 1 Series A, 1 Pre-Seed, and 2 Seed. This reflects a sector graduating from formation to scale. Companies like Stoïk (5th fundraise), Qevlar (2nd raise in under a year), and GitGuardian are on established growth trajectories.

### 4. Geopolitics and regulation are tailwinds
Rising U.S.-EU tensions, the enforcement of the EU AI Act, NIS2 directive requirements, and escalating state-sponsored cyber threats are creating structural demand. The global cyber insurance market is projected to reach $29B by 2027 (Statista). Every compliance mandate generates procurement budgets that flow to security vendors.

### 5. Integrated models win
The biggest funded companies aren't point solutions — they're platforms. Stoïk integrates insurance + prevention + response. Qevlar covers the full SOC workflow from triage to intelligence. GitGuardian spans secrets detection + NHI security + remediation. Escape covers discovery + testing + remediation. The market is rewarding end-to-end approaches.

## Outlook

French cybersecurity is no longer a niche within the broader tech ecosystem. It is emerging as a nationally strategic sector with:

- **Venture-scale capital** now flowing consistently
- **AI as a structural catalyst** for both threat creation and defense innovation
- **U.S. market access** as the defining growth strategy
- **Regulatory tailwinds** from EU AI Act, NIS2, and rising compliance mandates
- **A maturing company pipeline** graduating from seed to growth

The question for Q2 and beyond: can these companies convert U.S. traction into dominant market positions, or will they remain European champions in an American-dominated industry? The funding is there. The product innovation is there. The execution test is next.
